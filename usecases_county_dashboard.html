2012 Drought

You're a farmer in Sullivan County, Missouri. It's early June 2012, and your corn is struggling. Leaves curling, soil bone dry. But is this just your farm, or is something bigger happening?

You open the US Corn Belt Monitoring & Yield Forecasting dashboard. The vapor pressure deficit maps show atmospheric dryness at 90+ percentile across nearly every county in the Corn Belt. This pattern emerged in late May and is intensifying.

You realize: this isn't a local problem. This is a national drought disaster.

Your local elevator calls with a contract offer at current market prices.

But you have information they don't. The dashboard shows this drought is everywhere. National supply will crater. Prices will rise.

You hold. You don't panic contract.

By August, corn prices hit new records. You were right.

We can't go back to the past, but we can prepare for future conditions like this.

2020 Derecho

You manage a grain elevator in McLean County, Illinois. It's August 11, 2020. News reports show a derecho devastated Iowa yesterday. Your phone is quiet. Your corn is fine.

You open the US Corn Belt Monitoring & Yield Forecasting dashboard. The satellite data from August 11, just one day after the derecho, tells the story: over 20 Iowa counties show severe drops in the Green Chlorophyll Index, a measure of vegetation density and health. The damage is quantified and mapped. But Illinois, Indiana, Nebraska all show normal conditions.

You realize: Iowa's surplus just shrunk. Their basis will strengthen. The regional balance just shifted.

Just a week later, you check the dashboard again. The county-level yield forecasts for Iowa have dropped sharply, against previous record yield expectations. National yield expectations may fluctuate downward as well. The earlier bullish outlook needs to be reconsidered.

With the dashboard, you have the same intelligence big commodity firms have. You can see what's coming before the market fully adjusts.

You know your position just improved. Iowa elevators will be scrambling. Local basis might widen in your favor. You have time to think strategically while others are still assessing damage.

We can't go back to the past, but we can prepare for future conditions like this.

2024 Record Yields

You run a hog operation in Story County, Iowa. It's mid-July 2024, and you need to lock in corn contracts for the coming year's feed supply.

Your feed supplier calls: "Prices are decent right now. You should lock in contracts for the next 12 months before they rise."

But you open the US Corn Belt Monitoring & Yield Forecasting dashboard first. The crop conditions are fine. The county-level yield forecasts keep trending upward week after week.

You realize: this is shaping up to be a record year. Massive supply is coming.

You check back every two weeks through August and September. The forecasts hold strong. Iowa projecting record yields. Illinois, Indiana, Nebraska all excellent. National forecast climbing toward 183 bushels per acre.

You think there is no need to rush. You wait.

By October, harvest confirms the record yields. Feed corn prices remain favorable. You lock in contracts at better prices than July, saving on your largest operational cost.

We can't go back to the past, but we can prepare for future conditions like this.
